Fallout: New Vegas - Character Creation guide

Jan Inggao

April 7, 2022

Fallout: New Vegas is a classic game preceding the success of Fallout 3. A majority of New Vegas is built off of Fallout 3’s engine and game aspects. Creating your character in New Vegas shares some similarities such as “SPECIAL” and skills, while also adding a new mechanic; traits. While you may be confused about how to use these elements, hopefully this guide can give some clarity of how to utilize them.

Special:

After creating your character’s appearance in Fallout: New Vegas, you will be soon introduced to your character's SPECIAL stats. Each letter represents an attribute about your character, and symbolizing these characteristics:

  • Strength
  • Perception
  • Endurance
  • Charisma
  • Agility
  • Luck

Your SPECIAL primarily affects certain skills and perks. Additionally, SPECIAL can also affect other aspects of the game. You can set the value of each stat anywhere between 1 to 10. By default, all of these values are set to 5 with an extra 5 points for you to assign. You can relocate a SPECIAL’s stat value in order to get better use of a certain stat. The following table shows the skills affected and any additional effects.

SPECIAL Stat Skills Affected Additional Effects
Strength Melee weapons, Unarmed Melee damage, Carry weight, Weapon effectiveness
Perception Energy weapons, Explosives, Lockpick Enemy detection, Certain dialog options with NPC’s
Endurance Survival Hit points, Passive healing, Elemental resistance (Poison / Radiation)
Charisma Barter, Speech Companions armor and damage
Intelligence Medicine, Repair, Science Skill points gained per level up
Agility Guns, Sneak V.A.T.S. Action points, draw/holster speed, reload speed.
Luck All Skills Critical strike chance, Casino odds

Generally, most people like to invest their extra SPECIAL points into endurance and intelligence. Endurance allows you to take more damage, Intelligence allows you to spend more skill points on your character whenever you level up.

Skills:

Certain items and interactions in the world of New Vegas will be affected by your “skills”. Ranging from 1 to 100 skill points are given to the character whenever they level up. At the start you are given 3 “tag skill” choices. When you choose a skill to “tag”, that skill will gain an immediate boost of 15 points as well as additional items upon character completion. Your skill points level will determine how proficient you are in that skill field. The following table shows the skills in the game and some of their properties:

Skill Affected by SPECIAL stat Ability Extra items given when tagged
Barter Charisma Items are cheaper for the player and sell more
Explosives Perception Increase combat effectiveness with energy weapons + disarm explosives X8 dynamite (When “Guns” or “Energy Weapons” are not tagged)
Guns Agility Increase combat effectiveness with guns class 9mm pistol with x13 extra rounds
Lockpick Perception Every 25 skill point intervals lets you pick harder locks X12 bobby pins
Medicine Intelligence Increase healing benefits with medical tools and chemicals
Melee Weapons Strength Increase combat effectiveness with melee weapons Straight Razor
Repair Intelligence Repairs more durability on weapons
Science Intelligence Every 25 skill point intervals lets you hack harder terminals + Unlocks certain craftables the higher the level
Sneak Agility Better at staying undetected and stealing
Speech Charisma Certain dialog options are open
Survival Endurance Unlocks certain craftables the higher the level + increase certain food benefits
Unarmed Strength + Endurance Increase combat effectiveness with unarmed weapons Boxing gloves (When “melee weapons” are not tagged)

When choosing which skills to tag, generally only one combat skill should be focused on. These skills are considered combat skills ranked from best to worst:

  1. Guns
  2. Energy Weapons
  3. Melee Weapons
  4. Unarmed
  5. Explosives

Guns gives the player the most options to use in combat. Energy weapons are scarce but are powerful. Melee weapons and unarmed have some unique weapon choices but struggle with ranged fights. Explosives do massive damage, but also have the chance of hurting the player as well. Having lockpick or science as your second tag lets you access more loot. The last tag is up to the user but having repair as a last choice is a good option to make sure your weapons don’t break.

traits:

Traits are the last topic for character creation. Traits add a unique mechanic to your character, having both an advantage and disadvantage. The player gets to choose two traits at the beginning of the game. Overall, the traits don't affect too much in the game. some notable choices are:

Trait Pros Cons
Build to Destroy +3% critical hit chance Weapons break 15% faster
Fast shot Guns and energy weapons shoot 20% faster Guns and energy weapons are 20% less accurate
Good Natured Gain 5 skill points to Speech, Medicine, Repair, Science, and Barter skills Lose 5 skill points in Energy Weapons, Explosives, Guns, Melee Weapons, and Unarmed
Skilled Gain 5 points to every skill earn 10% fewer experience points
Wild Wasteland Adds additional 'wacky' content and modifies existing content and special encounters Removes some content
